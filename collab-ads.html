A two-phase redesign where earning retailer trust through friction fixes made a larger structural change possible.

Collaborative Ads ↗ lets brands run ads that direct buyers to their product pages on a their retailer's website or app. Before a brand can run any ads, they go through a joint onboarding process with their retailer, and that process was broken in ways neither side could easily fix. Most brands discovered the product only when a retailer personally reached them, waited weeks for manual review, and still weren't set up to run their first ad by the end of it.

This was a two-phase project. Phase 1 fixed the friction points breaking the existing process. Phase 2 rebuilt it from scratch and gave brands the ability to onboard themselves for the first time.

This project is under NDA. Screens shown here have shipped publicly. Full case study is available on request.

Phase 1: Friction fixes

I joined the team when a solution was already scoped. After running a UX audit and reviewing research, I reframed the scope around the gaps that were actually breaking the experience and got leadership buy-in to change build direction. In this phase, we shipped improvements to notifications, permissions assignment and collaboration term enforcement. Highlights shown below:

Automate where safe, hold where it matters

Ad account permissions stay manually assigned because they carry spend and billing access.

Catalog segment permissions, which let a brand's team use retailer products in ads, are auto-granted to anyone with ad account access.1

Phase 2: Brand initiated onboarding

In this phase, I led the vision to restructure onboarding to be brand-initiated, removing the retailer as the middle man to remove structural bottlenecks that were limiting new brand acquisition. I co-pitched the concept to our top retailer partners2 to validate the idea and define scope. Post-feedback, I designed and shipped a fully self-serve experience, from discovery to first ad, for brands collaborating with those retailers. Highlights shown below:

One entry point for all brands

Brands select their retailer upfront, and the form adapts from there. Routes to either a self-serve flow or a collaboration request depending on the retailer. The same entry point works for brands, or anyone acting on the brand's behalf.

A direct path to the first ad

Once setup is complete, brands land on a new Overview tab with a direct entry point to run a Collaborative Ads campaign with defaults applied. Next steps surface pending actions and best practices so nothing gets missed.

Outcomes

Phase 1 exceeded its onboarding completion goal. Phase 2 exceeded its daily revenue goal at launch. More importantly, our largest retailer partner removed eligibility gating and made Collaborative Ads available to all brands on their platform due to reduced manual effort to onboard brands.

Key Takeaway

Phase 1 wasn't just friction fixes. It was about earning the credibility to ask for something structurally different. Trust is the precondition for structural change.
